# Go-To-Market

## Target Customers

AMP is built for studios and teams that:

- Ship competitive multiplayer modes (1v1, small-team, or arena-style PvP).
- Want trustless on-chain stakes or rewards without building their own infra.
- Care about verifiable outcomes and replays, especially for tournaments or wagers.
- Prefer Avalanche (or are already there) and want infra that is ecosystem-native.

Primary initial segments:

- Indie and mid-size Web3 game teams building on Avalanche.
- Hackathon teams and experimental titles that need a fast way to add PvP stakes.
- Web2.5 studios experimenting with on-chain prizes or wagering in existing games.

---

## Phased Go-To-Market Plan

### Phase 1 – Builder GTM (0–6 months)

Focus: developers, hackathons, and early adopters.

- Position AMP as “matchmaking + settlement infra you can integrate in a day.”
- Run through Avalanche developer Discord and other ecosystem channels.
- Offer a free Fuji tier, hands-on integration support, and bounties for the first public integrations.
- KPIs: number of integrated games, weekly matches on Fuji, active devs in the AMP Discord.

### Phase 2 – Studio GTM (6–18 months)

Focus: small and mid-size studios with real player bases.

- Convert successful early integrations into case studies.
- Offer integration support packages and discounted early subscriptions.
- Co-market launches with Avalanche ecosystem partners and studios.
- KPIs: subscribed studios, MRR from subscriptions, monthly active players across AMP games.

### Phase 3 – Ecosystem & Subnet (18+ months)

Focus: AMP as core Avalanche gaming infra.

- Launch an AMP gaming subnet optimized for high-frequency match settlement.
- Support tournaments, leagues, and third-party tooling built on AMP transcripts.
- Explore governance and community-driven decisions around fee levels and verifier sets.
- KPIs: matches per day, subnet transaction volume, number of third-party tools built on AMP data.

---

## Revenue & Sustainability Model

Example tiers (subject to change):

- **Dev / Free** – Fuji-only, rate-limited, community support.
- **Indie** – Mainnet access with capped daily matches and standard SLAs.
- **Studio** – Higher match volumes, priority support, and roadmap influence.
- **Enterprise** – Custom terms, dedicated verifier instances, and co-marketing.

---

## Why AMP Can Win

- **Avalanche-native**: designed specifically for Avalanche C-Chain today, with a clear path to a dedicated gaming subnet tomorrow.
- **Developer-first**: language- and engine-agnostic SDKs, clear docs, and a simple “integrate in a day” experience.
- **Performance-focused**: Cap’n Proto-based match messaging and deterministic transcripts keep the infra fast enough for real games.
- **Composability**: shared replay/transcript standards make it easy for tools, analytics, and tournaments to plug in across many games.
